Strategic Insights
Resource Outlook 🌊

The 2028-2029 "Construction Wave"

There is a massive concentration of project starts scheduled for 2028 and 2029. Nearly 60% of the Clean Energy projects (Highland Valley, K2, Mount Mabel, Nilhts'i) are all targeting this 24-month window for construction. This suggests a potential localized labor and equipment bottleneck in the Thompson-Okanagan and Northeast regions.

Employment Mix 🛠️

Mining vs. Energy Sustainability

Energy and Gas projects are construction-heavy with massive initial job peaks but low permanent operational staffing. Conversely, Mining projects provide a much higher ratio of long-term operational jobs (e.g., Blackwater #12 provides 1,200 permanent roles). Mining remains the core driver of community-level economic stability.

Regional Hubs 📍

Regional Specialization

Thompson-Okanagan has rapidly evolved into B.C.'s Clean Energy Hub, hosting 45% of renewable projects. Meanwhile, the North Coast serves as the Industrial Gateway, hosting massive infrastructure (NCTL) and the province's largest Natural Gas processing and export developments.

Reconciliation 🤝

Indigenous Leadership

A defining feature of the "Look West" strategy is First Nations partnership. 100% of the Clean Energy projects list First Nations as primary or joint proponents. The transition to a clean economy in B.C. is moving beyond consultation into direct ownership and equity participation by Indigenous communities.
